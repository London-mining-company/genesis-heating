## Genesis Heating Solutions

### 1. Cash Flow Risk: "The CAPEX Hole"
**Risk:** Installing multiple "Managed Path" units at $0 down creates a significant cash flow deficit ($4,500 per unit).
- **Mitigation:**
    - **The 50/50 Rule:** Aim for a 1:1 ratio between Ownership Path (Upfront) and Managed Path ($0 down) customers.
    - **Activation Fees:** The $250 Service Activation Fee covers immediate HVAC contractor costs, reducing "Day 1" drain.
    - **Staged Rollout:** Limit "Managed" installs to 2 per month until the first 8 units achieve breakeven (Month 4).

### 2. Market Risk: Bitcoin Volatility
**Risk:** A significant drop in Bitcoin price reduces the value of mining rewards, extending the breakeven period.
- **Mitigation:**
    - **Efficiency First:** The Superheat H1 is one of the most efficient ASIC-to-thermal converters; it remains profitable at lower price points than standard miners.
    - **The Utility Hedge:** Position the product as an *effective* cost reducer. Even if rewards drop, the heat generated still replaces a paid utility (hot water).
    - **Service Fee Adjustment:** The Service Agreement allows Genesis to adjust the reward share slightly during "High Difficulty" periods to maintain infrastructure viability.

### 3. Operational Risk: Hardware Failure
**Risk:** An H1 unit fails, leaving a customer with no hot water and Genesis with a repair bill.
- **Mitigation:**
    - **Hybrid Plumbing:** Units should be installed with a standard bypass or as a pre-heater to an existing tank where possible, ensuring hot water continuity.
    - **Maintenance Escrow:** A portion of the "Phase I" revenue is held in a maintenance fund.
    - **Authorized Service:** Genesis acts as the authorized local partner, with direct access to Superheat's hardware support.

### 4. Technical Risk: The "Halving" Event
**Risk:** Every 4 years, Bitcoin rewards are cut in half, impacting the revenue share.
- **Mitigation:**
    - **Loyalty Scaling:** Phase III (35% Genesis share) is designed to handle halving events while keeping the customer's "free heating" status intact.
    - **Hardware Lifecycles:** Time hardware upgrades to coincide with halving cycles to maintain hash-rate efficiency.

### 5. Regulatory/Legal Risk
**Risk:** Changes in Ontario electrical codes or local utility policies.
- **Mitigation:**
    - **ESA Compliance:** Every installation is performed by a licensed HVAC tech and follows ESA (Electrical Safety Authority) standards.
    - **Straight Talk:** Avoid "investment" language. Position as a "Utility Management Service."
